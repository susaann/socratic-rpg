## 6.3 Parameters vs. Hyperparameters

**Parameters** are learned from data by gradient descent: weight matrices W(l)\mathbf{W}^{(l)}W(l), bias vectors b(l)\mathbf{b}^{(l)}b(l). The optimizer adjusts them automatically.

**Hyperparameters** are chosen by the practitioner before training begins:

  * _Architecture hyperparameters_ : number of layers LLL, layer widths d1,…,dLd_1, \ldots, d_Ld1​,…,dL​, activation functions
  * _Training hyperparameters_ : learning rate η\etaη, batch size BBB, number of epochs, optimizer (SGD/Adam/AdamW)
  * _Regularization hyperparameters_ : weight decay coefficient λ\lambdaλ, dropout rate



The parameter count formula (from Chapter 4): ∑l=1Ldl(dl−1+1)\sum_{l=1}^L d_l(d_{l-1} + 1)∑l=1L​dl​(dl−1​+1).

**Historical scale of parameters** :

Model | Year | Parameters  
---|---|---  
LeNet-5 | 1998 | ~60K  
AlexNet | 2012 | ~60M  
ResNet-152 | 2015 | ~60M  
BERT-large | 2018 | 340M  
GPT-3 | 2020 | 175B  
  
Parameters grew roughly 10× every two years — a scaling trend that has continued to drive improvements in model capability.
