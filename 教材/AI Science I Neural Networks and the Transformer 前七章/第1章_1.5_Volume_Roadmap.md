## 1.5 Volume Roadmap

This volume is organized into six Parts, each introducing a hypothesis class and explaining exactly why the previous hypothesis class was insufficient.

**Part I (Chapters 1–5)** : The general learning framework. Loss functions, gradient descent, multi-layer networks, and backpropagation. This is the engine that drives every architecture in the series.

**Part II (Chapters 6–9)** : Deep networks in practice. Skip connections, batch normalization, convolutional networks. The first paper close read: ResNet (He et al., 2015).

**Part III (Chapters 10–12)** : Sequences, memory, and gates. Recurrent networks and the LSTM. The second paper close read: LSTM (Hochreiter & Schmidhuber, 1997).

**Part IV (Chapters 13–16)** : Words as vectors. Distributed word representations, the distributional hypothesis. Paper close reads: Word2Vec (Mikolov et al., 2013) and GloVe (Pennington et al., 2014).

**Part V (Chapters 17–20)** : The road to attention. Encoder-decoder architectures, the information bottleneck, and the attention mechanism. Paper close reads: Seq2Seq (Sutskever et al., 2014) and Bahdanau attention (Bahdanau et al., 2015).

**Part VI (Chapters 21–26)** : The Transformer. Self-attention, multi-head attention, positional encoding, the complete architecture, and the pretraining paradigm. The central paper close read: "Attention Is All You Need" (Vaswani et al., 2017).

The volume's central question — _why does the Transformer look the way it does?_ — is answered only in Chapter 24, because answering it requires understanding every architectural decision that came before.
