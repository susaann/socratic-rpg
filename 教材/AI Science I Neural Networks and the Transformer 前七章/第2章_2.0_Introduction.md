> **Chapter Learning Objectives**
> 
> By the end of this chapter you should be able to:
> 
>   1. Articulate the three-element framework — hypothesis space, loss function, optimizer — and explain why each element is independently necessary
>   2. Derive the bias-variance decomposition from first principles and identify which model choices control each term
>   3. Prove the equivalence between minimizing mean squared error and maximizing a Gaussian likelihood
>   4. Explain regularization as a prior probability in a Bayesian framework
>   5. Distinguish _prediction_ (minimizing expected loss) from _statistical inference_ (recovering causal structure)
>
