# Chapter 7: The Challenge of Depth — Stabilizing Training

> **Chapter Learning Objectives**

By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:

  1. Derive the matrix product structure of gradient flow through deep networks and explain why exponential decay or explosion is inevitable without intervention.
  2. State the precise conditions under which gradients vanish or explode, using spectral radius analysis.
  3. Derive the Batch Normalization forward pass from first principles and distinguish its behavior in training vs. inference mode.
  4. Explain why Batch Normalization solves the internal covariate shift problem and compute its gradient contribution in backpropagation.
  5. Derive the Dropout regularization mechanism and interpret it as implicit ensemble averaging.
  6. Apply the bias-variance diagnostic framework to identify overfitting vs. underfitting from train/validation loss curves.
  7. Compare early stopping, weight decay, and dropout as regularization strategies, explaining the mathematical mechanism of each.
