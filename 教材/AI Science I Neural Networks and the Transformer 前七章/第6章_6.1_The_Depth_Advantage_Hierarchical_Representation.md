## 6.1 The Depth Advantage: Hierarchical Representation

Chapter 4 proved that nonlinearity is necessary for depth to add expressive power. Now we ask: how exactly does depth help, beyond the theoretical efficiency argument?

**Depth adds abstraction stages.** Each layer takes the previous layer's features as its "raw data" and extracts higher-level structure from them. The key property is **representation reuse** : a feature learned at layer 2 (say, an edge detector) is used by multiple neurons at layer 3, each combining it with other features in different ways. One basic component contributes to exponentially many compound patterns.

This compositionality matches the structure of natural data. Images are compositional: pixels form edges, edges form textures, textures form parts, parts form objects. Language is compositional: characters form morphemes, morphemes form words, words form phrases. A network that can learn hierarchically can exploit this structure efficiently.

**The exponential efficiency argument** (Chapter 4) can be quantified: for the class of functions computable by depth-O(k)O(k)O(k) networks with polynomially many parameters, a depth-O(1)O(1)O(1) network requires exponentially many parameters. Depth is not decoration — it is a form of structural compression that matches compositional data.
