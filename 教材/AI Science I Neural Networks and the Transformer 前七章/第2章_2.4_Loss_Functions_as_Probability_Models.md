### 2.4.3 Choosing a Loss Function

The probabilistic interpretation tells you how to choose a loss function: ask what noise model is appropriate for your data.

Data type | Noise model | Loss function  
---|---|---  
Real-valued, Gaussian noise | N(0,ŌÉ2)\mathcal{N}(0, \sigma^2)N(0,ŌÉ2) | Mean squared error  
Real-valued, heavy-tailed noise | Laplace distribution | Mean absolute error  
Binary outcome | Bernoulli | Binary cross-entropy  
Multiclass outcome | Categorical | Cross-entropy  
Count data | Poisson | Poisson deviance  
  
Choosing MSE for a heavy-tailed distribution (e.g., financial returns) is a coherent choice only if you believe the tails are Gaussian ‚ÄĒ if they are not, MSE assigns enormous weight to outliers (because the squared error of an outlier is disproportionately large). Mean absolute error corresponds to a Laplace noise model and is more robust.
