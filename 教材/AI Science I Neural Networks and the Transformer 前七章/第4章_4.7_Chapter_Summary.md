## 4.7 Chapter Summary

The perceptron established the template for learnable neurons, but a single linear layer cannot compute any function requiring a non-linear decision boundary — proved rigorously by the XOR impossibility result. Stacking linear layers without nonlinearity adds zero expressive power, because compositions of linear maps are linear. Nonlinear activation functions are what make depth meaningful.

ReLU transformed deep learning by solving the vanishing gradient problem: its derivative is 1 in the positive half, letting gradient signal flow without attenuation. GELU improves on ReLU with a smooth, differentiable gate that works particularly well in Transformer architectures. The choice of activation function encodes implicit assumptions about the data and the optimization landscape.

The Universal Approximation Theorem guarantees that a single hidden layer with enough neurons can approximate any continuous function — but this existence guarantee tells us nothing about how to find the approximating parameters or whether they will generalize. Depth provides exponential efficiency over width for functions with compositional structure, which explains why practical deep learning favors many layers over one very wide layer.
