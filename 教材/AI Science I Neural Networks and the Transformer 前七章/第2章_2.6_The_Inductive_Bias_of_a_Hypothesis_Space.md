## 2.6 The Inductive Bias of a Hypothesis Space

Every hypothesis space H\mathcal{H}H embeds assumptions about the structure of the function we are trying to learn. These assumptions are called the **inductive bias** of the model.

A linear model has strong inductive bias: it can only represent linear functions. This is a severe restriction, but on problems where the true relationship is approximately linear, it is a virtue — the model cannot overfit to noise.

A neural network with enough capacity has weak explicit inductive bias in the function-class sense, but every architectural choice introduces implicit inductive bias:

  * Convolutional layers assume _translation equivariance_ : a cat in the top-left corner should be recognized the same as a cat in the bottom-right corner
  * Recurrent layers assume _sequential dependence_ : the current state depends on past states
  * Transformer attention assumes _pairwise relevance_ : any two positions can directly influence each other



Understanding the inductive bias of an architecture is understanding _what structural knowledge about the problem is baked into the model_. When an architecture works well, it is partly because its inductive bias matches the structure of the data.

> **Cross-Disciplinary Connection: Inductive Bias in Physics, Linguistics, and Computational Biology**
> 
> **Physics** builds in inductive bias through symmetry constraints. Newton's laws are covariant under Galilean transformations; Maxwell's equations under Lorentz transformations. The Standard Model is derived by requiring invariance under U(1)×SU(2)×SU(3) gauge symmetries. When physicists build neural networks for particle physics, they constrain the architecture to be equivariant under the Lorentz group — this is not decoration, it is the correct inductive bias for the domain.
> 
> **Linguistics** encodes inductive bias in formal grammars. A context-free grammar (Chomsky, 1956) assumes hierarchical constituency structure; a dependency grammar assumes head-dependent relations. Each grammar formalism is a hypothesis space for natural language. The debate over which grammar best captures human language is precisely a debate over which inductive bias is correct.
> 
> **Computational biology** uses domain knowledge as inductive bias in protein structure prediction. AlphaFold2 uses an equivariant attention architecture that is explicitly constrained to be invariant to rigid-body transformations of the protein backbone — because protein function does not depend on the molecule's position in space, only its internal geometry. The correct inductive bias reduced the data requirement by orders of magnitude compared to unconstrained models.
